The five eras

Five thousand years of Avara

Each era mirrors a real period in economic history — from ancient trade to artificial intelligence.

Era I Ancient Kingdom

The Kingdom of Sundara

"The Golden Fields"

A benevolent king rules a land where trade is just beginning. Farmers discover surplus. Weavers connect villages. Markets form from nothing. A just ruler learns that his greatest power is facilitation — not extraction.

Era II Colonial Period

The Iron Ledger

"The Occupation of Sundara"

The Valdris Empire invades. Farmlands are seized. Industries extracted. But local merchants learn their colonisers' accounting, and farmers form the first trade unions. Ownership, regulation, and resistance begin to take shape.

Extractive Institutions Property Rights Labour Unions Resistance Economics
→
Era III Early Republic

The First Ledger

"The Republic of New Avara"

Independence is won. Trade unions become a government. The new republic must build institutions from scratch — negotiate its first trade deals, attract foreign investment, and protect infant industries from the world rushing in.

Institution Building Comparative Advantage FDI Infant Industry Fiscal Policy
→
Era IV Globalisation

Fragments & Fortunes

"The Age of Chains"

Avara is now a node in a planetary production network. Your shirt is assembled in six countries. Multinationals chase the cheapest labour. Privatisation reshapes everything. Profit maximisation becomes the new religion — and the middle class pays.

Global Value Chains MNCs Privatisation Financialisation Inequality
→
Era V The AI Age

The Algorithm Throne

"Who Owns the Future?"

Algorithms replace routine work. Platforms own the economy. Data is the new gold mine. In this era, the only capital that matters is human — creativity, judgment, adaptability. But who decides what the algorithm values?

Automation Platform Economics Data as Capital Future of Work UBI
